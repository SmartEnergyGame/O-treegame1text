Instructions Ecology

In this study, you will be in a randomly formed group of {{ Constants.players_per_group }} participants. Each participant in the group is given {{ Constants.endowment }}. The group has the opportunity to undertake a joint project. Each participant in the group decides how much she or he is going to contribute to the project. Contribution could be any integer from 0 to {{ Constants.endowment }}.
In this experiment, you are part of a community living in a shared apartment complex. You normally use {{ Constants.endowment }} units of energy a month, which provides you an equivalent amount of quality of life. To represent this quality of life, you will receive {{ Constants.endowment }} “energy units” or EU’s in each round of this experiment, and you will be paid $.25 for each EU you invest in your own energy use in each round. Thus, if you simply dedicate all of your EU’s to your own energy needs in every round, you will be paid $30 at the end of the game ($0.25 per EU x {{ Constants.endowment }} EUs x 12 months = $30.00)
In addition, your community has a goal to reduce its collective energy consumption by 20% over the next 12 months. Every month you have the opportunity to contribute toward this goal by reducing your energy consumption and “donating” 1 or more EU’s toward the group goal, lowering your own personal benefits as represented by your final payment in the experiment. For example, if you donate 2 EU’s toward the group energy reduction goal each month, you will reduce your final payment at the end of the experiment by 2 x $0.25 or $0.50 per month, or $6 for the full experiment.
